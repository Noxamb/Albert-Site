About Me

Hello! I’m Albert Einstein, a theoretical physicist best known for developing the theory of relativity — one of the two pillars of modern physics, alongside quantum mechanics. My work has also influenced the philosophy of science, and I’m perhaps most recognized for the equation E = mc², which expresses the relationship between mass and energy. Throughout my life, I’ve been driven by curiosity and a deep desire to understand the mysteries of the universe. You can learn more about my life and contributions on my Wikipedia page.
